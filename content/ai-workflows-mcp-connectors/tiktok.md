## #01 — Meeting → Action Items

**Hook (0–3s)**
"Your meeting ended 10 minutes ago. Who's writing down the action items right now?"

**Problem (3–10s)**
Every meeting ends with vague promises. Someone says "I'll follow up." Nobody writes it down. Nothing moves.

**Solution (10–30s)**
Here's the workflow. Otter records and transcribes the meeting. When it ends, AI reads the transcript, finds every action item, pulls the owner and the deadline, and creates the task in Notion or Todoist automatically. Before you've opened another tab.

**Close (30–40s)**
No developer. Afternoon of setup. It just runs.

## #02 — Email → CRM Update

**Hook (0–3s)**
"Sales reps spend a fifth of their day updating a CRM. That's not selling. That's data entry."

**Problem (3–10s)**
Prospect replies to your email. Now someone has to open the CRM, find the deal, update the stage, log the interaction, write a follow-up. Every. Single. Time.

**Solution (10–30s)**
The workflow: Gmail detects the reply. AI reads the thread, figures out what happened and what the prospect's intent is, updates the deal stage in HubSpot or Salesforce, logs the interaction, and queues a follow-up draft. Rep opens the CRM and it's already done.

**Close (30–40s)**
The rep's job is to sell. Not to update fields.

## #03 — Payment → Bookkeeping

**Hook (0–3s)**
"Every time a payment comes in, someone has to go touch the accounting software. Why?"

**Problem (3–10s)**
Reconciliation is the kind of work that feels fine when you're on top of it and becomes a nightmare when you fall behind. And it falls behind constantly.

**Solution (10–30s)**
Stripe fires a webhook when a payment succeeds. AI reads the payment, matches it to the product and customer record, categorizes it, and creates the invoice entry in QuickBooks or Xero. If something doesn't match cleanly, it flags it for review. Everything else just goes through.

**Close (30–40s)**
Your accountant stops doing data entry. They start doing accounting.

## #04 — Alert → Incident Briefing

**Hook (0–3s)**
"It's 2am. Something broke. Your on-call engineer just woke up. How long before they even know what they're looking at?"

**Problem (3–10s)**
Usually 10 minutes of tab-switching before they can even start diagnosing. Datadog. Deploy logs. Service map. Slack history. All manual, all while the thing is down.

**Solution (10–30s)**
The workflow: Datadog detects the anomaly. AI pulls the correlated signals, recent deploys, and affected services and writes a briefing. PagerDuty opens the incident. Slack post hits the on-call channel before the engineer opens their first tool. They wake up to context.

**Close (30–40s)**
The first 10 minutes of every incident used to be orientation. Now it's diagnosis.

## #05 — Signed Contract → Onboarding Project

**Hook (0–3s)**
"Client just signed. Who's building the onboarding project right now?"

**Problem (3–10s)**
Someone has to read the contract, extract the scope and key dates, build the project structure, assign the tasks. That work sits in an inbox for days. Every onboarding delay starts here.

**Solution (10–30s)**
PandaDoc registers the signature. AI reads the contract, pulls scope, timeline, and stakeholders, and creates the onboarding project in Asana or Linear — tasks, milestones, owners, pre-filled. By the time anyone checks, it's already built.

**Close (30–40s)**
The client signed. The project already exists. That's the experience you want to deliver.

## #06 — Prospect Research → Personalized Outreach

**Hook (0–3s)**
"Cold emails that open with 'I came across your profile' don't convert. Here's what does."

**Problem (3–10s)**
Generic outreach ignores everything that makes the prospect specific. Their recent post. Their company's hiring pattern. The announcement they just made. Most people don't have time to research every target. So they send something generic.

**Solution (10–30s)**
The workflow: drop a name into Apollo or pull their LinkedIn. AI reads their role, recent activity, company signals. Draft comes back opening on something real — a specific post, a move they made, a pain point their job listings are telegraphing. Subject line included.

**Close (30–40s)**
Personalization at scale. Not a mail merge. Actual context.

## #07 — New File → Knowledge Base Entry

**Hook (0–3s)**
"How many useful documents are sitting in a shared Google Drive folder that nobody has read twice?"

**Problem (3–10s)**
Files get uploaded. Nobody knows they're there. No index. No summary. No signal. The institutional knowledge lands and immediately disappears.

**Solution (10–30s)**
Workflow: file lands in a designated Drive folder. AI reads it and writes a structured summary. Notion page gets created with the summary, the source link, and the relevant tags. Findable the same day it was uploaded.

**Close (30–40s)**
Your Drive stops being a graveyard. Your knowledge base actually works.
