Strategic AI Adoption

Two Approaches to AI Integration

You can focus on technology or culture. RUDI integrates both - with a human-centered approach.

Cultural Axis

People-Centric Approach
  • Reduces fear and demystifies AI
  • Embeds tools into daily workflows
  • Builds literacy through learning
Focus: How people learn and adopt AI responsibly

Technical Axis

System-Centric Approach
  • Aligns tools, data, infrastructure
  • Ensures compliance & ethics
  • Modernizes metrics & ROI
Focus: Infrastructure and system optimization

RUDI Integrates Both Approaches. We address both the cultural and technical dimensions of AI adoption, but with a human-centered approach. We build comfort and literacy first, then layer in the governance and infrastructure.

Proficiency Scale

Five Levels of Mastery

Applied across each of the three dimensions

0

No Exposure

Conceptual: Never heard of generative AI / Operational: Never opened an AI tool / Governance: No awareness of AI risks

1

Pre-Beginner

Conceptual: Heard "AI" or "ChatGPT" but can't explain / Operational: Can open tools but unsure what to type / Governance: Knows AI may be biased but not how

2

Beginner

Conceptual: Can describe AI as "a tool that generates text/images" / Operational: Can try basic prompts, recognize when output is off / Governance: Can name risks like privacy, misinformation, bias

3

Intermediate

Conceptual: Understands training data, bias, why outputs vary / Operational: Uses AI for work tasks with cross-checking / Governance: Knows policies, applies them correctly

4

Advanced

Conceptual: Can critically discuss AI's societal role, ethics, limitations / Operational: Designs AI workflows, mentors peers / Governance: Leads conversations on AI policy, ethics, adoption

Responsible Use

The Responsible Use Model

How to use AI safely and effectively in practice

Beyond technical competency, RUDI teaches responsible AI use through a framework that answers: What data can I use? How carefully should I check outputs? What questions should I ask?

Habits of Responsible Use

The questions you ask
  • Source Tracing - Where did this come from?
  • Cross-Referencing - Does this hold up elsewhere?
  • Perspective Testing - Whose voice is present/missing?
  • Boundary Marking - What are the limits?
  • Bias Awareness - What assumptions shape this?

Levels of Scrutiny

How carefully to check
1

Light Touch

Low-stakes tasks, internal brainstorming

2

Standard Review

Client-facing content, published work

3

Deep Verification

Legal, compliance, high-stakes decisions
